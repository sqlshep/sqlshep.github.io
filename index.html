The Millennium Project was originally commissioned by the UN in 2002 and chose 8 Goals, their mission was to reduce hunger, poverty, disease, increase access to education for 177 countries in extreme need. The program continued until 2007 when the program was integrated into the United Nations Development Program.

From this project, MYWorld was introduced, a global survey for citizens led by the United Nations and and over 600 partners. Its aim was to capture people's voice, priorities and views, so that global leaders can be informed as they begin the process of defining the new development agenda for the coming 15 years.

Started in 2013, through creative online and offline methods, MYWorld asked individuals which six of sixteen possible issues they think would make the most difference in their lives. The sixteen issues have been built up from the priorities expressed by poor people in existing research and polling exercises and they cover the existing Millennium Development Goals, plus issues of sustainability, security, governance and transparency.



Survey results were firstly submitted to the Secretary General´s High Level Panel for Post-2015 before their meetings in Monrovia, Bali and New York and drove their reccomendations in the final report for a new development framework.

From ths data in 2013 a new set of 12 goals was selected by the UN Post 2015 High-Level Panel. The survey continued through the end of 2015 and is actually still accepting responses even today. The results of this most recent survey were used to define the direction for the next 15 years, until the year 2030.

Afterwhich, MY World will continue gathering people´s voices up to 2015 and results will be shared with the Secretary General and global leaders as they prepare the next development agenda in the run up to 2015.

Nine and a half million votes were collected across 195 countries with assistance from over 600 organizations. The visualizations below can be used to explore the data.

(HDI) Human Development Index: A composite index measuring average achievement in three basic dimensions of human development—a long and healthy life, knowledge and a decent standard of living. See Technical note 1 at http://hdr.undp.org/en for details on how the HDI is calculated.
(GNI) Gross national income per capita: Aggregate income of an economy generated by its production and its ownership of factors of production, less the incomes paid for the use of factors of production owned by the rest of the world, converted to international dollars using PPP rates, divided by midyear population.
